Use monster cards

These are invoked to attack the opponent's life points and defend yours. They are usually orange (effect) or yellow (normal), but there are also many other colors. Monsters have levels, ranging from 1 to 12 and are indicated by the stars at the top and a symbol in the upper right corner indicating the attribute. Above the text of the card, the monster's type, class, and abilities (such as tuner or flip-flop) are written in bold. Attack and Defense stats are listed as ATK and DEF at the bottom.

Summon your monster to the field

A summon is a way to place a monster on your field. There are 3 main types of invocations: normal, special, and flip. A Normal Summon can be performed once per turn and there is no limit to Special Summons. You can perform a normal summon of a monster from your hand in a face-up attack position or a face-down defense position (known as a normal position). Monsters of level 4 or lower do not require tribute, but for those of higher level, you will have to send monsters from your field to the graveyard. Levels 5 and 6 require a tribute, and levels 7 and above require 2. A normal invocation that involves tributes is also known as a tribute invocation.

Use magic cards

These cards are greenish in color. They are usually triggered from your hand during your turn and have various effects. There are 6 different types of spells, and those that are different from normal will have an icon in the upper right near the bold text indicating their type.

Use trap cards

The cheats are intended to be used during the opponent's turn to interrupt their games. The traps are purple in color and have a symbol in the corner for anything other than a normal trap. All Trap Cards must be placed (face down in a Zone S or T) before they can be used, and can be activated during either player's turn.
